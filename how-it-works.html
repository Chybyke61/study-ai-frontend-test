The Platform Guide

Inside the Intelligence Engine

Study.AI isn't just a chatbot; it's a personalized RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) memory bank for your specific curriculum. Here is exactly how to use it.

1

Upload Your Curriculum

Begin by creating a new "Study Space." Upload your raw academic materials directly into the platform. This can include:

  • Heavy PDF Textbooks (e.g., Biology, Law, Engineering)
  • Lecture presentation slides
  • Typed personal notes and research papers

No generic web searches—the AI is strictly confined to the truths within the documents you provide.

2

Vector Indexing Phase

Once uploaded, you will see a progress bar as our system goes to work. Behind the scenes, Study.AI reads every single page and breaks your documents down into "embeddings."

This process creates a mathematical map of your textbook, allowing the AI to instantly fetch the exact paragraph needed to answer your questions later, even if the textbook is 1,000 pages long.

3

Chat & Extract Notes

When indexing is complete, open the Chat Interface. You can now talk directly to your materials. You can command the AI to:

> "Summarize chapter 4 into bullet points"

> "Explain the concept of 'mitosis' as defined by the author on page 45"

> "What are the key formulas I need to remember from this lecture?"
4

Automated Quizzes

Testing your memory is the best way to learn. Instead of manually writing flashcards, click the Generate Quiz button.

The AI will scan your indexed documents and instantly generate multiple-choice questions, fill-in-the-blanks, and short-answer prompts based entirely on the curriculum you uploaded.
